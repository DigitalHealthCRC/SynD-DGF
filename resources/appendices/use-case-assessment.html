Appendix 4

Use case assessment

Detailed criteria and guidance for assessing whether a proposed use case is appropriate for synthetic health data generation and use.

Assessment overview

Before proceeding with synthetic data generation, it's crucial to evaluate whether the proposed use case is appropriate and whether synthetic data can meet the intended objectives. This assessment helps ensure resources are used efficiently and that synthetic data is only generated when it can provide genuine value.

Core assessment criteria

Use case assessment should evaluate multiple dimensions to determine suitability for synthetic data approaches:

Purpose and objectives clarity

Confirm the proposed project is focused, measurable, and aligned with the unique strengths of synthetic data.

Weight 5

Clear research objectives

The intended use has well-defined, specific research or analytical objectives that can be evaluated for success.

Weight 4

Measurable outcomes

Success criteria can be clearly defined and measured using synthetic data outputs.

Weight 3

Appropriate scope

The scope is neither too narrow (where synthetic data adds no value) nor too broad (where precision is lost).

Technical feasibility

Ensure the data landscape, tooling, and validation pathways support dependable synthetic data generation.

Weight 5

Data complexity alignment

Source data relationships and structures can be modelled using available synthetic data techniques.

Weight 4

Quality requirements achievable

Required quality thresholds can realistically be achieved with current synthesis methods and resources.

Weight 3

Tooling & expertise

Appropriate platforms, infrastructure, and specialist skills are available to run generation and monitoring.

Weight 3

Validation feasibility

Robust validation methods exist to confirm synthetic outputs meet utility and privacy requirements.

Legal and ethical appropriateness

Confirm the use case respects privacy law, meets ethical expectations, and delivers tangible community benefit.

Weight 5

Lawful basis

There is a clear, documented legal basis for source data use and synthetic generation across all jurisdictions involved.

Weight 4

Ethical justification

Expected benefits clearly outweigh residual privacy risks and align with organisational ethical frameworks.

Weight 3

Community benefit

The project supports public value, cultural expectations, and stakeholder priorities, including Indigenous data considerations.

Alternative assessment

Validate that synthetic data is the most appropriate option compared with other pathways.

Weight 4

Real data inaccessible

Privacy, security, or logistical constraints make direct access to real-world data impractical or non-compliant.

Weight 3

Unique synthetic advantages

Synthetic data delivers clear benefits such as scale, reproducibility, or controlled risk environments.

Weight 2

No simpler alternative

Simpler solutions (aggregated statistics, open datasets, simulations) cannot satisfy the analytical objectives.

Decision flowchart

Follow this decision process to systematically evaluate use case appropriateness:

Step 1: Define objectives
Can you clearly articulate what you want to achieve and how success will be measured?
↓
Step 2: Legal pathway exists?
Is there a clear lawful basis for using source data to generate synthetic data for your purpose?
↓ YES
Step 3: Technical feasibility
Can synthetic data methods realistically produce data that meets your quality requirements?
↓
Step 4: Alternative assessment
Have you confirmed that real data access isn't feasible and that simpler approaches won't work?
↓ YES
Proceed with assessment
Continue to detailed source data assessment and synthetic data generation planning

Scoring methodology

Use this scoring system to evaluate use case appropriateness systematically:

Criteria Weight Score 3 (High) Score 2 (Medium) Score 1 (Low) Score 0 (Poor)
Weight 5 (Critical) 15 points 10 points 5 points 0 points
Weight 4 (Important) 12 points 8 points 4 points 0 points
Weight 3 (Moderate) 9 points 6 points 3 points 0 points
Weight 2 (Minor) 6 points 4 points 2 points 0 points

Interpretation guidelines

  • 80-100 points: Highly appropriate use case - proceed with confidence
  • 60-79 points: Appropriate use case - proceed with careful planning
  • 40-59 points: Marginal use case - consider alternatives or significant modifications
  • Below 40 points: Inappropriate use case - synthetic data not recommended

Note: Any critical criteria (weight 5) scoring 0 points should result in reconsideration regardless of total score.

Example assessments

These examples illustrate how the assessment criteria apply to different scenarios:

Highly Appropriate

Algorithm development for rare disease detection

Scenario: Developing machine learning algorithms to detect rare genetic conditions in newborn screening data.

Assessment rationale:

  • Clear objectives: Specific algorithmic performance targets for rare condition detection
  • Technical feasibility: Synthetic data can generate additional rare condition examples
  • Legal basis: Public health benefit with clear regulatory pathway
  • Alternatives limited: Real rare disease data is extremely limited and sensitive

Score: 85/100

Conditional Approval

Health system efficiency research

Scenario: Analysing patient flow patterns to optimise hospital resource allocation.

Assessment rationale:

  • Clear objectives: Well-defined efficiency metrics and improvement targets
  • Technical challenges: Complex temporal and operational relationships may be difficult to preserve
  • Legal basis: Health service improvement provides clear justification
  • Alternatives exist: Aggregated operational data might suffice for many analyses

Score: 65/100 - Proceed with enhanced validation requirements

Not Appropriate

General medical research training

Scenario: Creating synthetic patient data for general medical student training exercises.

Assessment rationale:

  • Unclear advantages: Educational objectives could be met with simplified, clearly artificial cases
  • Legal complexity: Using real patient data for synthetic generation may not be justified for general education
  • Alternatives available: Purpose-built educational datasets or simulated cases would be more appropriate
  • Scope too broad: General training doesn't require the complexity that necessitates synthetic data

Score: 25/100 - Use alternative approaches

Documentation requirements

Comprehensive documentation of the use case assessment is essential for governance and accountability:

Required documentation

Maintain a clear record explaining why synthetic data is appropriate and how the use case will be governed.

Use case definition

Document objectives, scope, analytical approach, success metrics, and key stakeholders.

Scoring rationale

Capture evidence for each criterion score, including data sources, expert advice, and assumptions.

Alternative analysis

Explain why alternative approaches (direct access, aggregated reporting, simulations) are insufficient.

Risk mitigation

Outline mitigations for privacy, technical, operational, governance, and community risks.

Governance sign-off

Record approvals from relevant oversight bodies before moving to data generation or sharing.
