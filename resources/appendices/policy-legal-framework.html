Australia's Privacy Law 'Patchwork'

The system of privacy regulation in Australia is best described as 'patchwork'. Given the range of current and potential future SynD members, a range of privacy laws may apply to a single synthetic data project.

Key challenges for synthetic data projects

  • Multiple privacy laws may apply to a single project depending on participating organisations
  • Federal and state/territory laws often overlap and may have different requirements
  • Private sector health providers may be subject to both Commonwealth and state privacy laws
  • Each privacy law contains similar but distinct privacy principles and exemptions

Jurisdictional privacy regimes

Quick-reference cards summarise which privacy instruments govern synthetic health data across the Commonwealth and every state and territory.

CTH Privacy Act 1988 (Cth)

13 Australian Privacy Principles

Applies to
  • Commonwealth government agencies (including public universities)
  • Private sector organisations nationally
  • All private and NGO health service providers
Coverage and notes

Regulates collection, use, disclosure, security, quality, and access rights for personal information.

Small business exemption: Entities below $3M turnover are exempt unless they provide health services — most SynD partners remain in scope.

ACT Information Privacy Act 2014

13 Territory Privacy Principles

Applies to

ACT public sector agencies, including ACT public universities.

Health records

Health Records (Privacy and Access) Act 1997 (12 Privacy Principles) regulates both public and private health service providers.

Important: Personal health information is excluded from the general IP Act — use the HRPA Act rules for synthetic health data projects.

NSW NSW privacy legislation

12 IPPs · 15 Health Privacy Principles

General privacy law

Privacy and Personal Information Protection Act 1998 applies to NSW public sector agencies, including universities.

Establishes 12 Information Protection Principles for non-health personal information.

Health information law

Health Records and Information Privacy Act 2002 covers health information held by public agencies and private health service providers.

Introduces 15 Health Privacy Principles that sit alongside APP requirements for cross-jurisdictional projects.

NT Information Act 2002

10 Information Privacy Principles

Scope

Applies to NT public sector agencies, government business divisions and NT public universities.

Sets out 10 IPPs covering collection, use, disclosure, and data quality for personal information.

QLD Information Privacy Act 2009

13 Queensland Privacy Principles

Scope

Covers Queensland government agencies and public universities, with dedicated rules for health agencies.

Provides 13 QPPs aligned closely with the APPs, ensuring consistent obligations for inter-jurisdictional projects.

SA Information Privacy Principles Instruction

10 SA Information Privacy Principles

Scope and status

Applies to South Australian public sector agencies despite not being primary legislation.

Important: Policy is issued under Premier and Cabinet Circular 12 and enforced via the Privacy Committee of South Australia.

TAS Personal Information Protection Act 2004

10 Personal Information Protection Principles

Scope

Applies to Tasmanian public sector agencies and public universities.

Places obligations on collection, storage, use, and disclosure similar to the APPs, providing a baseline for synthetic data governance.

VIC Victorian privacy regime

10 IPPs · 11 Health Privacy Principles

General privacy law

Privacy and Data Protection Act 2014 applies to Victorian public sector agencies and universities.

Establishes 10 IPPs addressing personal information handling.

Health privacy law

Health Records Act 2011 covers public and private sector health service providers.

Introduces 11 Health Privacy Principles that mirror the NSW framework for health information.

WA Privacy and Responsible Information Sharing Act 2024

11 WA Information Privacy Principles

Scope

Applies to WA public sector organisations, including universities, and introduces the state's first comprehensive privacy framework.

Implementation: Privacy provisions are expected to commence in 2026; planning for compliance should begin now.

Information sharing framework

Creates a responsible information sharing scheme enabling formal agreements for government data — critical when synthetic datasets draw from WA sources.

Additional considerations

  • Other relevant laws: Organisations may also be subject to other laws that impact data handling, such as the Public Health Act 2010 (NSW)
  • Organisational policies: SynD members may have their own policies that apply to the creation and handling of synthetic data, which must be applied alongside this framework
  • General privacy principle: As a very general statement, privacy laws typically prohibit the disclosure of personal information, unless an exception applies
  • Common structure: Each privacy law contains a set of privacy principles covering similar ground: collection, use and disclosure of personal information, data security, data quality, access and correction rights
