Appendix 11

Assessment Outcomes Forms

Comprehensive documentation forms and templates for recording assessment outcomes, decisions, and audit trails throughout synthetic health data projects, ensuring accountability and regulatory compliance.

Documentation accountability framework

Assessment outcomes documentation provides the essential bridge between evaluation activities and governance accountability. These forms capture not just what decisions were made, but why they were made, who made them, and how they align with project objectives and regulatory requirements.

Proper documentation enables continuous improvement, supports regulatory submissions, facilitates external audits, and ensures that assessment knowledge is preserved and transferable across project teams and organisations.

Assessment outcomes documentation system

The documentation system comprises eight integrated components that ensure comprehensive coverage of all assessment activities and outcomes:

Use case assessment outcome forms
Document use case evaluation results, feasibility determinations, and alignment with organisational objectives.
Impact assessment documentation templates
Record privacy impact assessment findings, risk evaluations, and mitigation strategy decisions.
Technical assessment reporting forms
Capture technical evaluation results, quality metrics, and performance validation outcomes.
Safety assessment outcome records
Document safety evaluation findings, risk assessments, and ongoing monitoring requirements.
Decision recording and audit trail systems
Maintain comprehensive records of all assessment decisions with supporting rationale and evidence.
Quality assurance and review documentation
Track quality assurance activities, peer reviews, and continuous improvement processes.
Storage and archiving management
Ensure appropriate retention, access control, and retrieval of assessment documentation.
Governance integration and reporting
Connect assessment outcomes with broader governance processes and stakeholder reporting requirements.

Use case documentation requirements
  • Complete assessment methodology and criteria applied
  • Detailed stakeholder consultation records
  • Strategic alignment evaluation with supporting evidence
  • Risk-benefit analysis with quantitative measures where possible
  • Decision rationale with reference to assessment criteria
  • Conditions, requirements, or recommendations for implementation
  • Review and approval sign-offs from appropriate authorities

Impact assessment integration

Impact assessments must be integrated across privacy, social, ethical, and technical domains. Isolated assessments may miss important interactions between different types of impacts, particularly where technical choices affect privacy outcomes or where social considerations influence technical design requirements.

Technical assessment quality assurance
  1. Independent validation using different tools and methodologies
  2. Peer review of technical assessment procedures and results
  3. Domain expert validation of clinical or scientific plausibility
  4. Cross-validation against known benchmarks and standards

Safety assessment ongoing requirements

Safety assessment is not a one-time activity. The outcome documentation must establish clear ongoing monitoring requirements, review schedules, and trigger events for safety reassessment. This includes changes in data use, new user groups, technical infrastructure updates, or emerging safety threats.

Safety outcome documentation standards
  • Comprehensive risk register with ongoing updates
  • Quantitative safety testing results and metrics
  • Independent safety assessment validation
  • Clear safety approval conditions and limitations
  • Detailed monitoring and review requirements
  • Incident response procedures and contact information
  • Integration with broader organisational safety frameworks

Long-term accessibility considerations

Assessment documentation may need to be accessible for many years after project completion for audit, appeal, or research purposes. Storage solutions must account for format evolution, system migrations, and ongoing accessibility requirements while maintaining security and integrity.

Governance integration requirements
  • Regular reporting schedules aligned with governance meeting cycles
  • Integration with broader risk management and compliance reporting
  • Coordination with ethics committees and oversight bodies
  • Alignment with project management and delivery governance
  • Connection to strategic planning and resource allocation processes
  • Integration with external regulatory and audit requirements
  • Stakeholder engagement and communication coordination
Governance reporting best practices
  1. Tailor reporting content and format to audience needs and expertise
  2. Provide clear executive summaries with key decision points highlighted
  3. Include quantitative metrics and benchmarks where appropriate
  4. Present balanced view including both achievements and challenges
  5. Provide clear recommendations with supporting rationale
  6. Ensure consistency across different reporting channels and audiences
  7. Follow up on governance decisions and track implementation progress

Using these assessment outcome forms

These assessment outcome forms provide the documentation backbone for synthetic data governance. When implementing these forms:

  • Adapt templates to match your organisational requirements and governance structures
  • Ensure consistency across different assessment domains while allowing for domain-specific requirements
  • Establish clear completion standards and quality assurance procedures before using forms
  • Train assessment teams on proper form completion and documentation requirements
  • Integrate forms with existing organisational systems and workflows
  • Regularly review and update forms based on experience and changing requirements
  • Maintain appropriate balance between comprehensiveness and usability
