Critical Issues

Step 1: Use case assessment compliance

Ensure all use case assessment requirements are met before proceeding to Step 2.

Framework requirement: Complete Step 1 compliance before proceeding to source data assessment

Public benefit assessment

Clear documentation of how project serves public benefit related to health services, system improvement, policy development, or research.

Specific identification of who will benefit from the project outcomes (patients, providers, researchers, policymakers).

Documented consideration of alternatives (aggregation, traditional de-identification) and justification for synthetic data approach.

Community notification

Public communication about synthetic data use for public benefit, including transparency about methods and purposes.

Process for receiving and responding to community concerns about data governance approach.

Impact assessment

Comprehensive evaluation of ethical implications including fairness, transparency, and proportionality.

If Indigenous health data is involved, consultation with ACCHOs and compliance with Indigenous data governance principles.

Additional safeguards for vulnerable populations (children, mental health, disability, socioeconomically disadvantaged).

Step 2: Source data assessment compliance

Ensure proper assessment and preparation of source data with appropriate safeguards.

Framework requirement: Complete Step 2 compliance before synthetic data generation

Data governance

All necessary approvals from data custodians, site governance, and ethics committees.

Signed agreements covering data access, use restrictions, security requirements, and disposal procedures.

Review that existing consent covers synthetic data generation and intended uses, or new consent obtained.

Technical safeguards

Source data encrypted at rest and in transit with appropriate key management.

Role-based access controls limiting data access to authorized personnel only.

Comprehensive logging of all data access, modifications, and processing activities.

Step 3: Synthetic data generation compliance

Ensure synthetic data generation meets quality, privacy, and technical standards.

Framework requirement: Complete Step 3 compliance before risk assessment

Generation standards

Chosen generation method validated as appropriate for data type and intended use case.

Systematic evaluation of synthetic data quality using appropriate statistical and utility measures.

Appropriate privacy protection techniques integrated into generation process (differential privacy, k-anonymity, etc.).

Documentation requirements

Comprehensive documentation of generation approach, parameters, and configuration.

Detailed report of quality metrics, limitations, and fitness for intended use.

Step 4: Re-identification risk assessment compliance

Systematic evaluation and documentation of re-identification risks and vulnerabilities.

Framework requirement: Complete Step 4 compliance before residual risk management

Risk assessment

Systematic identification of potential attack scenarios and threat actors.

Quantitative or qualitative assessment of re-identification risk levels across different scenarios.

Testing of synthetic data against known re-identification attack methods.

Risk documentation

Formal register of identified risks with severity ratings and mitigation strategies.

Assessment of potential consequences for individuals and organisation if re-identification occurs.

Step 5: Residual risk management compliance

Implementation and monitoring of ongoing risk management and governance measures.

Framework requirement: Complete Step 5 compliance for project approval and ongoing governance

Risk mitigation

Comprehensive plan addressing all identified risks with specific mitigation measures and responsibilities.

Ongoing monitoring procedures for data use, access patterns, and potential security incidents.

Documented procedures for responding to suspected privacy breaches or re-identification attempts.

Ongoing governance

Ongoing governance structure with defined roles, responsibilities, and oversight mechanisms.

Scheduled periodic reviews of compliance status, risk levels, and governance effectiveness.

Cross-cutting compliance requirements

Requirements that apply across multiple framework steps and throughout the project lifecycle.

Legal and regulatory

Ongoing compliance with Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and applicable state/territory privacy laws.

Compliance with jurisdiction-specific health data legislation (HRIP Act NSW, Health Records Act Vic, etc.).

Human Research Ethics Committee approvals current and conditions met (if applicable).

Documentation and records

Comprehensive records of all compliance activities, assessments, and decisions.

Complete audit trail of all data processing, synthetic data generation, and access activities.

Regular communication with relevant stakeholders about project progress and compliance status.

Compliance assessment report

Comprehensive compliance status with priority actions and recommendations.

Compliance management guidance

Using this checklist effectively

  • Complete relevant sections before each framework step milestone
  • Update checklist regularly as project progresses
  • Address mandatory items before proceeding to next step
  • Document evidence for each completed item
  • Schedule regular compliance reviews

Requirement levels explained

  • Mandatory: Must be completed to proceed - legal/framework requirement
  • Conditional: Required if specific conditions apply to your project
  • Recommended: Best practice - strongly advised for comprehensive governance
