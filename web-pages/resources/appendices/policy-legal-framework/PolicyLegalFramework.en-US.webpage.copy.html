Australia's Privacy Law 'Patchwork'

The system of privacy regulation in Australia is best described as 'patchwork'. Given the range of current and potential future SynD members, a range of privacy laws may apply to a single synthetic data project.

Key challenges for synthetic data projects

  • Multiple privacy laws may apply to a single project depending on participating organisations
  • Federal and state/territory laws often overlap and may have different requirements
  • Private sector health providers may be subject to both Commonwealth and state privacy laws
  • Each privacy law contains similar but distinct privacy principles and exemptions
CTH
Commonwealth privacy laws
Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) 13 APPs

Applies to:

  • Commonwealth government agencies (including Commonwealth public universities)
  • Private sector organisations
  • All private sector and NGO health service providers across Australia

Coverage: Australian Privacy Principles (APPs) regulate the collection, use and disclosure of personal information, as well as data security, data quality, access and correction rights.

Note: There is an exemption for private sector organisations with a turnover of less than $3M pa, but this exemption does not apply to 'health service providers'. This means that even very small NGO and community organisations which offer health services will be regulated by the APPs.
ACT
Australian Capital Territory
Information Privacy Act 2014 (ACT) 13 TPPs

Applies to: ACT public sector agencies (including ACT public universities)

Coverage: 13 Territory Privacy Principles (TPPs)

Important: The IP Act does not apply to the handling of 'personal health information' about individuals, which is instead regulated by the HRPA Act.
Health Records (Privacy and Access) Act 1997 (ACT) 12 PPs

Applies to: Both public sector and private sector health service providers

Coverage: 12 Privacy Principles (PPs) specifically for health information

NSW
New South Wales
Privacy and Personal Information Protection Act 1998 (NSW) 12 IPPs

Applies to: NSW public sector agencies (including NSW public universities)

Coverage: 12 Information Protection Principles (IPPs)

Health Records and Information Privacy Act 2002 (NSW) 15 HPPs

Applies to:

  • NSW public sector agencies (including NSW public universities)
  • All private sector health service providers

Coverage: 15 Health Privacy Principles (NSW HPPs)

NT
Northern Territory
Information Act 2002 (NT) 10 IPPs

Applies to: Northern Territory public sector agencies (including NT public universities)

Coverage: 10 Information Privacy Principles (NT IPPs)

QLD
Queensland
Information Privacy Act 2009 (QLD) 13 QPPs

Applies to: Queensland government agencies (including Queensland public universities)

Coverage: 13 Queensland Privacy Principles (QPPs)

SA
South Australia
Premier and Cabinet Circular 12 - Information Privacy Principles Instruction 10 IPPs

Applies to: South Australian public sector agencies

Coverage: 10 Information Privacy Principles (SA IPPs)

Important: There are no specific privacy laws in South Australia. This Instruction creates a binding policy for public sector agencies, but it is not law and cannot be enforced by a court. The Instruction creates the Privacy Committee of South Australia, which handles privacy complaints relating to the SA IPPs. Last re-issued in May 2020.
TAS
Tasmania
Personal Information Protection Act 2004 (Tas) 10 PIPPs

Applies to: Tasmanian public sector agencies (including Tasmanian public universities)

Coverage: 10 Personal Information Protection Principles (PIPPs)

VIC
Victoria
Privacy and Data Protection Act 2014 (Vic) 10 IPPs

Applies to: Victorian public sector agencies (including Victorian public universities)

Coverage: 10 Information Privacy Principles (IPPs)

Health Records Act 2011 (Vic) 11 HPPs

Applies to:

  • Victorian public sector agencies (including Victorian public universities)
  • Private sector health service providers

Coverage: 11 Health Privacy Principles (Vic HPPs)

WA
Western Australia
Privacy and Responsible Information Sharing Act 2024 (WA) 11 IPPs

Applies to: WA public sector organisations (including WA public universities)

Coverage: 11 Information Privacy Principles (WA IPPs) that apply to the handling of personal information (including health information)

Implementation note: Until very recently, Western Australia did not have comprehensive privacy legislation to regulate how the WA public sector handles personal information. It is anticipated that the privacy provisions in the PRIS Act will commence in 2026.

Additional framework: The PRIS Act also creates a responsible information sharing legislative framework that governs the handling of WA government data. Under this framework, information sharing agreements can be formed under the terms of the PRIS Act, giving legal authority for the collection, use and disclosure of government information.

Summary of applicable Australian privacy laws regulating health information

Where privacy laws establish specific obligations for the handling of 'health information' or 'sensitive information' (as a distinct category of 'personal information') – either through separate legislation or a specific set of privacy principles – these are the relevant privacy obligations that will apply to the handling of health data.

Additional considerations

  • Other relevant laws: Organisations may also be subject to other laws that impact data handling, such as the Public Health Act 2010 (NSW)
  • Organisational policies: SynD members may have their own policies that apply to the creation and handling of synthetic data, which must be applied alongside this framework
  • General privacy principle: As a very general statement, privacy laws typically prohibit the disclosure of personal information, unless an exception applies
  • Common structure: Each privacy law contains a set of privacy principles covering similar ground: collection, use and disclosure of personal information, data security, data quality, access and correction rights
