Privacy obligations regulating the use & disclosure of health information

A comprehensive matrix outlining privacy laws and lawful pathways for the use and disclosure of health information across all Australian jurisdictions.

About this matrix

This appendix provides a detailed analysis of privacy obligations across all Australian jurisdictions, including Commonwealth, state, and territory privacy laws. It outlines the specific circumstances under which health information can be lawfully used or disclosed for secondary purposes, including research and synthetic data projects.

CTH
Privacy Act 1988 (Commonwealth)

Applicable organisations

  • Commonwealth government agencies
  • Private sector organisations (including private universities and private sector health organisations), with some exemptions
  • Non-government organisations

Relevant privacy principles

Australian Privacy Principle 6 (APP 6)

Lawful pathways for use or disclosure

  • Primary purpose use
    For the primary purpose it was collected (APP 6.1)
  • Secondary purpose within reasonable expectations
    For a directly related secondary purpose within the patient's reasonable expectations (APP 6.2(a)(i))
  • With consent
    With the individual's consent (APP 6.1(a))
  • Required by law
    If the use or disclosure is required or authorised under another law (APP 6.2(b))
  • Research exemption (Section 16B(3))
    Where the use or disclosure is necessary for research, or the compilation or analysis of statistics, relevant to public health or public safety, and:
    • It is impracticable to obtain the individual's consent to the use or disclosure
    • The use or disclosure is conducted in accordance with NHMRC's guidelines issued and approved under s 95A of the Privacy Act (which may require HREC approval)
    • In the case of a disclosure, the organisation reasonably believes that the recipient of the information will not disclose the information (or personal information derived from the information)
  • Public Interest Determination (PID)
    Where the Privacy Commissioner makes a Public Interest Determination that provides the organisation is not required to comply with the privacy principle requirements that usually apply (Part VI, Div 1)
Reference: NHMRC Guidelines under Section 95A
ACT
Health Records (Privacy and Access) Act 1997

Applicable organisations

  • ACT government agencies (including ACT public universities)
  • Public and private health organisations in the ACT

Relevant privacy principles

Privacy Principles 9 & 10 (PP 9, PP 10)

Lawful pathways for use

  • Primary purpose use
    For the primary purpose it was collected (PP 9(1))
  • Treating team sharing
    Where the information is being shared between members of the individual's treating team for health treatment purposes (PP 9(1))
  • With consent
    Where the individual has consented to the use (PP 9(1)(a))
  • Required by law
    Where the use is required or authorised under another law (PP 9(1)(c))
  • Directly related purpose
    Where purpose for the use is directly related to the primary purpose of collection (PP 9(1)(d))
  • Health service management
    The use of the information is related to the management, funding or quality of the health service received by the individual (PP 9(1)(e))

Lawful pathways for disclosure

  • Treating team sharing
    Where the information is being shared between members of the individual's treating team for health treatment purposes (PP 10(2)(a))
  • Reasonable awareness
    Where the individual is reasonably likely to have been made aware that their information will be disclosed to the particular entity (PP 10(2)(b))
  • Written consent
    With the individual's written and signed consent (PP 10(2)(c) & PP 10(7))
  • Required by law
    The disclosure is required or authorised under another law (PP 10(2)(e))
  • Health service management
    Where the disclosure of the information is necessary for the management, funding or quality of the health service received, or being received, by the individual (PP 10(2)(f))
  • Research in public interest (PP 10(3))
    Where the disclosure is necessary for the purpose of research or the compilation or analysis of statistics, in the public interest, and:
    • It is impracticable to seek the individual's consent before disclosure
    • The purpose of the research or the compilation or analysis of statistics cannot be achieved by disclosing de-identified data
    • The receiving entity is required to:
      • Protect the health information to at least the same standard set out under the HRPA Act (ACT) and prevent any further disclosure
      • Take reasonable steps to de-identify or destroy the health information at the earliest possible opportunity
      • Ensure that identifiable health information is not made publicly available
    • The disclosure is made in accordance with any relevant guidelines issued under the HRPA Act (which may require HREC approval)
Important note

Where the disclosure of health information is allowed under any of these exceptions, the recipient entity must not use or disclose the information for a purpose other than the purpose for which the information was provided (PP 10(8)).

NSW
Health Records and Information Privacy Act 2002

Applicable organisations

  • NSW government agencies (including NSW public universities)
  • Public and private health organisations in NSW

Relevant privacy principles

Health Privacy Principles 10, 11 & 14 (HPP 10, HPP 11, HPP 14)

Lawful pathways for use or disclosure

  • Primary purpose
    For the primary purpose it was collected (HPP 10(1); HPP 11(1))
  • With consent
    With the individual's consent (HPP 10(1)(a); HPP 11(1)(a))
  • Secondary purpose within reasonable expectations
    For a directly related secondary purpose within the individual's reasonable expectations (HPP 10(1)(b); HPP 11(1)(b))
  • Required by law
    If the use or disclosure is required or authorised under another law (HPP 10(2); HPP 11(2))
  • Health services management (HPP 10(1)(d); HPP 11(1)(d))
    For the funding, management, planning or evaluation of health services, and:
    • Either this purpose cannot be achieved with de-identified data and it is impracticable to seek the individual's consent for the use or disclosure OR reasonable steps have been taken to de-identify the information
    • Identifiable health information will not be published in a generally available publication
    • The use or disclosure is in accordance with NSW Privacy Commissioner's statutory guidelines on the management of health services (which may require HREC approval)
  • Research in public interest (HPP 10(1)(f); HPP 11(1)(f))
    For research, or the compilation or analysis of statistics, in the public interest, and:
    • Either this purpose cannot be achieved with de-identified data, and it is impracticable to seek the individual's consent for the use, or disclosure OR reasonable steps have been taken to de-identify the information
    • Identifiable health information will not be published in a generally available publication
    • The use or disclosure is in accordance with NSW Privacy Commissioner's statutory guidelines on research (which may require HREC approval)
  • Health Privacy Code
    Where a Health Privacy Code (prepared in consultation with the NSW Privacy Commissioner and approved by the NSW Health Minister) modifies the HPPs to authorise the use or disclosure of health information by organisations in the circumstances (ss 38 – 40)
  • Public Interest Direction (PID)
    Where the NSW Privacy Commissioner makes a Public Interest Direction that provides organisations are not required to comply with the HPPs in the circumstances (s 62)
Transfer restrictions (HPP 14)

Organisations are not permitted to transfer health information outside of NSW or to a Commonwealth agency unless specific conditions are met, including reasonable belief that the recipient is subject to substantially similar privacy protection, individual consent, or contractual necessity.

Reference: NSW Statutory Guidelines - Health Issue Management
NT
Information Act 2002

Applicable organisations

  • NT government agencies (including NT public universities)

Relevant privacy principles

Information Privacy Principles 2 & 9 (IPP 2, IPP 9)

Lawful pathways for use or disclosure

  • Primary purpose
    For the primary purpose it was collected (IPP 2.1)
  • Secondary purpose within reasonable expectations
    For a directly related secondary purpose within the individual's reasonable expectations (IPP 2.1(a))
  • With consent
    With the individual's consent (IPP 2.1(c))
  • Required by law
    If the use or disclosure is required or authorised under another law (IPP 2.1(f))
  • Research in public interest (IPP 2.1(ca))
    Where the use or disclosure is necessary for research, or the compilation or analysis of statistics, in the public interest, and:
    • It is impracticable to obtain the individual's consent to the use or disclosure
    • The research, compilation or analysis will not be published in a form that identifies the individual
    • The use or disclosure is conducted in accordance with the guidelines issued by the NT Information Commissioner (which may require HREC approval)
    • In the case of a disclosure, the organisation reasonably believes that the recipient of the information will not disclose the information
  • Code of Practice
    The organisation is subject to a Code of Practice approved by the NT Information Commissioner that modifies the IPPs to allow limited non-compliance (conditions must be met to support such modifications) (ss 72 – 80)
  • Public interest authorisation
    The NT Information Commissioner has granted a public interest authorisation to allow the organisation to use or disclose health information in a manner that would otherwise be inconsistent with the IPPs (s 81)
Transfer restrictions (IPP 9)

Similar to other jurisdictions, organisations are not permitted to transfer health information outside of the NT unless specific conditions are met, including authorisation by law, substantially similar privacy protection, individual consent, or contractual necessity.

Reference: NT Guidelines - Use and Disclosure of Health Information
VIC
Health Records Act 2011

Applicable organisations

  • Victorian government agencies (including Victorian public universities)
  • Public and private health organisations in Victoria

Relevant privacy principles

Health Privacy Principles 2 & 9 (HPP 2, HPP 9)

Lawful pathways for use or disclosure

  • Primary purpose
    For the primary purpose it was collected (HPP 2.1)
  • With consent
    With the individual's consent (HPP 2.2(b))
  • Secondary purpose within reasonable expectations
    For a directly related secondary purpose within the individual's reasonable expectations (HPP 2.2(b))
  • Required by law
    If the use or disclosure is required or authorised under another law (HPP 2.2(c))
  • Health service provision
    Where the use or disclosure is necessary for the provision of a health service to the individual (HPP 2.2(e)), including where the individual is incapable of giving consent (HPP 2.2(d))
  • Health services management (HPP 2.2(f)(i))
    Where the use or disclosure is for the purpose of funding, management, planning, monitoring, improvement or evaluation of health services, and:
    • Either this purpose cannot be achieved with de-identified data and it is impracticable to seek the individual's consent for the use OR reasonable steps have been taken to de-identify the information
    • Identifiable health information will not be published in a generally available publication
    • The use or disclosure is in accordance with any guidelines issued by the Victorian Health Complaints Commissioner on the management of health services
  • Research in public interest (HPP 2.2(g))
    Where the use or disclosure is necessary for research, or the compilation or analysis of statistics, in the public interest, and:
    • Is impracticable to seek the individual's consent for the use or disclosure
    • This purpose cannot be achieved with de-identified data
    • The use or disclosure is in accordance with the statutory guidelines on research issued by the Victorian Health Complaints Commissioner (which may require HREC approval)
    • Identifiable health information will not be published in a generally available publication
Transfer restrictions (HPP 9)

Organisations are not permitted to transfer health information outside of Victoria unless specific conditions are met, including substantially similar privacy protection, individual consent, contractual necessity, or authorisation by law.

Note: To date, no statutory guidelines have been issued by the Victorian Health Services Commissioner on the management of health services.
QLD
Information Privacy Act 2009

Applicable organisations

  • Queensland government agencies (including Queensland public universities)

Relevant privacy principles

Queensland Privacy Principle 6 (QPP 6)

Lawful pathways for use or disclosure

  • Primary purpose
    For the primary purpose it was collected (QPP 6.1)
  • Secondary purpose within reasonable expectations
    For a directly related secondary purpose within the individual's reasonable expectations (QPP 6.2(a)(i))
  • With consent
    With the individual's consent (QPP 6.1(a))
  • Required by law
    If the use or disclosure is required or authorised under another law (QPP 6.2(b))
  • Health agency research (Permitted Health Situation 4)
    Where the organisation is a health agency and the use or disclosure is necessary for research, or the compilation or analysis of statistics, relevant to public health or public safety, and:
    • It is impracticable to obtain the individual's consent to the use or disclosure
    • The use or disclosure is conducted in accordance with guidelines approved by the chief executive of the health department (which may require HREC approval)
    • In the case of a disclosure, the organisation reasonably believes that the recipient of the information will not disclose the information (or personal information derived from the information)
  • Research in public interest (QPP 6.2(g))
    Where the use or disclosure is necessary for research, or the compilation or analysis of statistics, in the public interest, and:
    • The use or disclosure does not involve the publication of all or any of the personal information in a form that identifies any individual
    • It is not practicable to obtain the consent of the individual before the use or disclosure
    • In the case of a disclosure, the organisation is satisfied on reasonable grounds the recipient of the information will not disclose the information
  • Public interest waiver
    The Queensland Information Commissioner approves a public interest waiver or modification of a privacy principle requirement that applies to the organisation (s 157)
De-identification requirement

If the health information was originally collected under Permitted Health Situation 3(1), the organisation must take reasonable steps to ensure that the information is de-identified before the organisation discloses it under QPP 6.1 or QPP 6.2 (QPP 6.4).

Note: We cannot see that any such guidelines have been approved to date under the QLD NPPs; however, guidelines relevant to this pathway may be issued after the QPPs become effective.
SA
Premier and Cabinet Circular 12 - Information Privacy Principles Instruction

Applicable organisations

  • South Australian government agencies

Note: This instruction is a piece of binding policy on South Australian government agencies. It is not law.

Relevant privacy principles

Information Privacy Principles 7-8 & 10 (IPP 7-8, IPP 10)

Lawful pathways for use or disclosure

  • Primary purpose
    For the primary purpose it was collected (IPP 8; IPP 10)
  • Related secondary purpose within reasonable expectations
    For a related secondary purpose within the individual's reasonable expectations (IPP 8(a); IPP 10(a))
  • With consent
    With the individual's consent (IPP 8(b); IPP 10(b))
  • Required by law
    If the use or disclosure is required or authorised under another law (IPP 8(d); IPP 10(d))
Privacy Committee exemption

The Privacy Committee of South Australia may also exempt an organisation from compliance with one or more of the IPPs (Proclamation of the Privacy Committee of South Australia, s 4(1)).

TAS
Personal Information Protection Act 2004

Applicable organisations

  • Tasmanian government agencies (including Tasmanian public universities)

Relevant privacy principles

Personal Information Protection Principles 2 & 9 (PIP 2, PIP 9)

Lawful pathways for use or disclosure

  • Primary purpose
    For the primary purpose it was collected (PIP 2.1)
  • Secondary purpose within reasonable expectations
    For a directly related secondary purpose within the individual's reasonable expectations (PIP 2.1(a))
  • With consent
    With the individual's consent (PIP 2.1(b))
  • Required by law
    If the use or disclosure is required or authorised under another law (PIP 2.1(f))
  • Research in public interest (PIP 2.1(c))
    Where the use or disclosure is necessary for research, or the compilation or analysis of statistics, in the public interest (other than for publication in a form that identifies any particular individual), and:
    • It is impracticable to obtain the individual's consent before the use or disclosure
    • In the case of a disclosure, the organisation reasonably believes that the recipient of the information is not likely to disclose the information
  • Public interest exemption
    Where the Minister for Justice has approved a public interest exemption from compliance with the PIP requirements (ss 13 – 15)
De-identification requirement for research data

Where the health information was originally collected under PIP 10.4 (i.e. for research or the compilation or analysis of statistics relevant to public health or public safety, or the management, funding or monitoring of a health service), the organisation must take reasonable steps to permanently de-identify the health information before disclosing it (PIP 10.5).

Transfer restrictions (PIP 9)

Organisations are not permitted to transfer health information outside of Tasmania unless specific conditions are met, including substantially similar privacy protection, individual consent, contractual necessity, or authorisation by law.

WA
Privacy and Responsible Information Sharing Act 2024

Applicable organisations

  • Western Australian government agencies (including Western Australian public universities)

Relevant privacy principles

Information Privacy Principle 2 (IPP 2)

Lawful pathways for use or disclosure

  • Primary purpose
    For the primary purpose it was collected (IPP 2.1)
  • Secondary purpose within reasonable expectations
    For a directly related secondary purpose within the individual's reasonable expectations (IPP 2.1(a))
  • With consent
    With the individual's consent (IPP 2.1(b))
  • Required by law
    If the use or disclosure is required or authorised under another law (IPP 2.1(f))
  • Research in public interest (IPP 2.1(c))
    Where the use is necessary for research, or the compilation or analysis of statistics, in the public interest, and:
    • The research or statistics are not to be published in a form that identifies any particular individual
    • It is impracticable to obtain the individual's consent to the use or disclosure
    • The organisation reasonably believes that the recipient of the information will not further disclose the information
  • Public Interest Direction (PID)
    Where the WA Information Commissioner makes a Public Interest Direction that provides the organisation is not required to comply with the privacy principle requirements in the circumstances (ss 45 – 48)
  • Information sharing agreements (Part 3)
    In the case of a disclosure, where the disclosure is to either another WA public sector entity, or to an 'external entity', for a 'permitted purpose' in accordance with a properly formed information sharing agreement. This includes:
    • External entities: Commonwealth, state or territory agencies; contracted service providers; Aboriginal community controlled organisations; bodies that carry out health-related research
    • Permitted purposes: informing government policy; managing government programs and services; research and development with clear public benefits
    • Must meet PRIS Act requirements including prior assessments (which may include responsible sharing principles, privacy impact assessment, and Aboriginal information assessment)

Using this matrix

This matrix provides a comprehensive overview of privacy obligations across all Australian jurisdictions. When planning synthetic data projects, identify your organisation type and applicable jurisdiction(s) to determine which privacy laws apply and what lawful pathways are available for your specific use case.

Consider engaging with legal experts familiar with health data privacy laws in your jurisdiction for specific guidance on complex scenarios.
