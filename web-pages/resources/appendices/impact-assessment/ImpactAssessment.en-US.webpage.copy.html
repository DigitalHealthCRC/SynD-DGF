Appendix 5

Impact assessment

Comprehensive methodology for assessing the privacy, ethical, social, technical, and legal impacts of synthetic health data projects.

Assessment framework overview

Impact assessment for synthetic health data projects requires evaluation across multiple domains to ensure comprehensive consideration of all potential consequences. This assessment should be conducted early in project planning and revisited throughout the project lifecycle.

Impact assessment matrix

Use this matrix to systematically evaluate potential impacts across all domains:

Impact Domain High Risk Medium Risk Low Risk Assessment Priority
Privacy Impact Re-identification possible Limited risk with controls Minimal privacy concerns Critical
Ethical Impact Significant ethical concerns Some ethical considerations Minor ethical implications High
Social Impact Potential for harm/discrimination Limited social concerns Positive social benefit High
Technical Impact Technical limitations significant Some technical challenges Technical approach sound Medium
Legal Impact Legal compliance unclear Some legal complexities Clear legal pathway Critical

Privacy impact assessment

Privacy impact assessment evaluates risks to individual privacy throughout the synthetic data lifecycle.

Key assessment areas

Source data privacy

Assessment of privacy risks in accessing and using source data for synthetic generation.

  • What sensitive information is contained in source data?
  • How will source data be accessed and stored?
  • Who will have access to source data during the project?
  • What controls prevent unauthorised source data access?
Generation process risks

Privacy risks arising during the synthetic data generation process itself.

  • Could the generation process inadvertently preserve identifiable information?
  • Are generation algorithms privacy-preserving by design?
  • How is model training data protected?
  • What quality assurance processes protect privacy?
Synthetic data disclosure risks

Evaluation of re-identification and inference risks in synthetic data outputs.

  • Could individuals be re-identified from synthetic data patterns?
  • What attributes or combinations pose re-identification risks?
  • How will synthetic data be distributed and accessed?
  • What controls limit inappropriate use of synthetic data?
Ongoing privacy protection

Long-term privacy protection measures and monitoring arrangements.

  • How will privacy protection be monitored over time?
  • What happens if privacy risks increase after generation?
  • How can synthetic data be recalled if necessary?
  • What privacy breach response procedures exist?

Ethical impact assessment

Ethical assessment ensures synthetic data projects align with fundamental ethical principles and community values.

Ethical evaluation framework

Autonomy and consent

Respect for individual autonomy and decision-making in synthetic data use.

  • How do consent arrangements address synthetic data generation?
  • Can individuals opt out of synthetic data generation?
  • Are people informed about synthetic data creation from their data?
  • How are incapacitated persons' interests protected?
Beneficence and non-maleficence

Ensuring benefits are maximised and harms minimised.

  • What specific benefits does this project aim to achieve?
  • Who benefits from the synthetic data use?
  • What potential harms could arise?
  • How do benefits compare to risks and resource use?
Justice and fairness

Fair distribution of benefits and burdens across different groups.

  • Are benefits and risks fairly distributed across population groups?
  • Could synthetic data perpetuate or amplify existing biases?
  • How are vulnerable populations protected?
  • What measures ensure equitable access to benefits?
Transparency and accountability

Open communication about synthetic data projects and clear accountability structures.

  • How will the project be communicated to stakeholders?
  • What information will be publicly available?
  • Who is accountable for project outcomes?
  • How can the community provide input or feedback?

Indigenous data sovereignty considerations

When synthetic data projects involve Indigenous health data, additional ethical and governance considerations apply, guided by the CARE Principles for Indigenous Data Governance.

Collective Benefit

Data ecosystems shall be designed and function in ways that enable Indigenous Peoples to derive benefit from the data.

Authority to Control

Indigenous Peoples' rights and interests in Indigenous data must be recognised and their authority to control such data be empowered.

Responsibility

Those working with Indigenous data have a responsibility to share how those data are used to support Indigenous Peoples' self-determination and collective benefit.

Ethics

Indigenous Peoples' rights and wellbeing should be the primary concern at all stages of the data lifecycle and across the data ecosystem.

Indigenous data assessment questions

Community engagement
  • Has meaningful consultation occurred with relevant Indigenous communities?
  • Do Indigenous communities support the synthetic data project?
  • How will ongoing community involvement be maintained?
Governance and control
  • Do Indigenous communities have appropriate governance roles?
  • How can communities exercise control over their data?
  • What mechanisms exist for community oversight?
Benefit sharing
  • How will Indigenous communities benefit from the project?
  • Are benefits shared equitably and appropriately?
  • How will project outcomes support community priorities?
Cultural sensitivity
  • Does the project respect Indigenous cultural values?
  • How are cultural protocols incorporated?
  • What cultural competency training is provided to staff?

Social impact assessment

Social impact assessment evaluates broader societal effects and community implications of synthetic data projects.

Social impact evaluation areas

Community trust and acceptance
  • How might the project affect public trust in health data use?
  • What community concerns need to be addressed?
  • How will community acceptance be built and maintained?
Equity and discrimination
  • Could synthetic data perpetuate health inequities?
  • How are minority and vulnerable groups represented?
  • What measures prevent discriminatory use of outputs?
Healthcare system impacts
  • How might project outcomes affect healthcare delivery?
  • Could synthetic data improve health system equity?
  • What changes to clinical practice might result?
Economic and resource implications
  • What are the economic costs and benefits?
  • How do resource requirements compare to alternatives?
  • What economic impacts on communities are expected?

Technical impact assessment

Technical assessment evaluates the feasibility, quality, and reliability of synthetic data generation approaches.

Technical evaluation criteria

Data quality and utility
  • Can synthetic data achieve required quality levels?
  • How will utility for intended purposes be validated?
  • What quality assurance processes will be implemented?
Technical feasibility
  • Are proposed methods technically achievable?
  • What technical expertise and resources are required?
  • How will technical challenges be addressed?
Scalability and sustainability
  • Can the approach scale to required data volumes?
  • How will ongoing technical maintenance be managed?
  • What happens if technical approaches become obsolete?
Security and integrity
  • How will data and system security be maintained?
  • What measures protect against technical attacks?
  • How is data integrity assured throughout the process?

Legal and regulatory impact

Legal assessment ensures compliance with applicable laws and regulations across all jurisdictions.

Legal compliance evaluation

Privacy law compliance
  • Which privacy laws apply to this project?
  • How do Australian Privacy Principles apply?
  • Are there state or territory specific requirements?
  • How do international privacy laws apply if relevant?
Health and research regulation
  • What health sector regulations apply?
  • Are there specific research governance requirements?
  • How do ethics approval processes apply?
  • What professional or industry standards are relevant?
Data sharing and transfer
  • What legal frameworks govern data sharing arrangements?
  • How do cross-border data transfer laws apply?
  • What contractual arrangements are required?
  • How are third-party obligations managed?
Liability and accountability
  • How are legal liabilities allocated between parties?
  • What insurance or indemnity arrangements apply?
  • How is legal accountability for outcomes managed?
  • What dispute resolution mechanisms exist?

Impact assessment workflow

Follow this systematic workflow to conduct comprehensive impact assessment:

1
Scoping and initial assessment

Define project scope, identify stakeholders, and conduct initial impact screening across all domains. Determine which impact areas require detailed assessment based on project characteristics.

2
Stakeholder engagement

Engage relevant stakeholders including affected communities, subject matter experts, and governance bodies. Gather input on potential impacts and mitigation strategies.

3
Detailed impact analysis

Conduct detailed assessment for each relevant impact domain using the criteria and questions provided. Document findings, risk levels, and areas requiring mitigation.

4
Mitigation strategy development

Develop specific strategies to address identified risks and negative impacts. Ensure mitigation measures are practical, effective, and proportionate to identified risks.

5
Review and approval

Present impact assessment findings and mitigation strategies to appropriate governance bodies for review and approval. Address any concerns or requirements for additional assessment.

6
Ongoing monitoring

Implement monitoring processes to track impacts throughout project lifecycle. Conduct periodic reassessment as project scope or context changes.

Special considerations and requirements

When enhanced assessment is required:

  • Sensitive populations: Projects involving children, Indigenous peoples, or other vulnerable groups require enhanced ethical and social impact assessment
  • Sensitive data types: Mental health, genomic, or highly stigmatised health conditions require enhanced privacy and ethical assessment
  • Commercial applications: Projects with commercial components require enhanced assessment of benefit distribution and community interests
  • Cross-border data use: International projects require enhanced legal and regulatory assessment across multiple jurisdictions
  • Novel methodologies: Use of new or experimental synthetic data methods requires enhanced technical and safety assessment
