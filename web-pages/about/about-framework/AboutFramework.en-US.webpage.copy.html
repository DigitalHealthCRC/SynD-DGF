About the SynD-DGF Framework

The Synthetic Data Governance Framework (SynD-DGF) is a comprehensive, evidence-based approach to navigating the complex legal, technical, and ethical landscape of synthetic health data in Australia.

Framework overview

The SynD-DGF addresses critical gaps in current governance approaches by providing practical, implementable guidance for organisations seeking to harness the benefits of synthetic health data while maintaining robust privacy protections and regulatory compliance.

Why synthetic data governance matters

  • Privacy protection: Synthetic data can enable research while reducing privacy risks, but only with proper governance
  • Regulatory compliance: Complex legal landscape requires systematic approach to ensure compliance
  • Indigenous data sovereignty: Specific considerations for Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander health data
  • Quality assurance: Framework ensures synthetic data maintains utility while protecting privacy
  • Risk management: Systematic identification and mitigation of reidentification risks

Framework scope and applicability

Intended users

  • Healthcare providers and health services
  • Research institutions and universities
  • Government health departments
  • Technology vendors and service providers
  • Privacy officers and legal teams
  • Data scientists and researchers

Data types covered

  • Clinical and medical records
  • Administrative health data
  • Public health surveillance data
  • Digital health and wearable data
  • Genomic and biomedical data
  • Mental health and wellbeing data

Regulatory alignment

The framework aligns with key Australian and international regulations:

  • Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) - Australian Privacy Principles compliance
  • Therapeutic Goods Administration - Medical device and software regulations
  • NHMRC Guidelines - Research ethics and governance requirements
  • State health privacy laws - Jurisdiction-specific requirements
  • GDPR and international standards - Global best practice alignment
