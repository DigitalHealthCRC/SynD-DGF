Use case assessment checklist

This mandatory assessment evaluates whether your use case meets the three fundamental requirements for synthetic health data generation under the SynD-DGF framework.

Critical requirement

Each of these three tests must be met in order for your synthetic data request to proceed to the next steps. If the Data Custodian is not satisfied that your request meets these tests, an alternative lawful privacy pathway must be determined.

Types of synthetic data requests

This assessment applies to any of the following scenarios:

  • One organisation requests another organisation to generate a synthetic dataset for a specific project
  • An organisation wishes to establish a synthetic dataset for multiple potential projects or purposes
  • An organisation (or End User) requests to access or use a synthetic dataset that was created for a different purpose or use case

Test 1: Clear 'public benefit' purpose

Requirement: The use case is for a clear 'public benefit' purpose related to providing health services, and where the expected benefits from the use case are related to consumer health or health system outcomes.

Framework requirement: Test 1 mandatory for Step 1 use case assessment progression
Framework-approved use cases include:
  • Research-related activities: Where the research is for a public benefit purpose, including conducting PoC analysis and feasibility testing, carrying out preliminary research tasks, or using PoC results to vet research proposals
  • Healthcare system management: Tasks related to the management, planning and funding of healthcare system activities, where the task does not require real data
  • Education and training: Activities related to analysing health data for beneficial purposes (such as hackathons that test models for generating insights into population health)
  • Healthcare technology development: Development and improvement activities that are for a clear public benefit purpose

Assessment guidance

Completing this assessment

  • Answer all questions honestly and comprehensively
  • Provide specific details rather than generic responses
  • Consider seeking input from relevant stakeholders
  • Document your reasoning for borderline decisions
  • Review your responses before finalising the assessment

If your assessment fails

If any test fails, consider these options:

  • Revise your approach: Modify the use case to better align with requirements
  • Enhance engagement: Improve community notification and consultation
  • Consider alternatives: Explore aggregated data or traditional de-identification
  • Seek guidance: Consult with privacy experts or ethics committees

Documentation requirements

Keep records of:

  • Completed assessment responses and reasoning
  • Evidence of public communication efforts
  • Stakeholder consultation outcomes
  • Alternative approaches considered and rejected
  • Any expert advice or ethical review outcomes
