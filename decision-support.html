The decision support system helps you when:

Non-standard pathways

Your project doesn't fit the standard 5-step framework pathway

Complex scenarios

Multi-organisational

Multiple organisations or jurisdictions are involved

Higher risk

Legal compliance

You need guidance on legal pathways and compliance requirements

Common complex scenarios

Examples of situations where decision support tools are particularly valuable

Multi-organisational projects

Data linkage across health systems

A research project requires linking data from multiple public hospitals and private health insurers to create a comprehensive synthetic dataset for health outcomes research.

Complexity factors:
  • Multiple data custodians with different privacy obligations
  • Need for data linkage before synthetic data generation
  • Different jurisdictional privacy law requirements
  • Complex consent and authorisation requirements

Recommended path: Complex scenarios navigator → Legal pathways wizard

Third-party service providers

Cloud-based synthetic data generation

A health department wants to use a specialised cloud-based synthetic data generation service to create datasets for policy development and service planning.

Complexity factors:
  • Source data must be shared with third-party service provider
  • International cloud infrastructure location issues
  • Additional privacy and security risk assessment required
  • Complex contractual and liability arrangements

Recommended path: Complex scenarios navigator → Risk mitigation planner

Indigenous health data

Aboriginal community health research

A university research team wants to generate synthetic data from Aboriginal Community Controlled Health Organisation (ACCHO) records for health disparity research.

Complexity factors:
  • Indigenous data sovereignty and self-determination principles
  • Community consultation and consent requirements
  • Cultural sensitivity and appropriate research methodologies
  • Specific HREC and ethics approval pathways

Recommended path: Complex scenarios navigator → Legal pathways wizard

High-risk datasets

Rare disease registry

A specialist clinic wants to create synthetic data from a rare disease patient registry to support international research collaboration while protecting patient privacy.

Complexity factors:
  • Very small population size increases re-identification risk
  • Highly distinctive clinical characteristics
  • International data sharing considerations
  • Patient advocacy group engagement requirements

Recommended path: Risk mitigation planner → Legal pathways wizard

When to seek expert consultation

Some scenarios may require consultation with subject matter experts beyond the decision support tools

Legal expertise

  • Novel legal interpretations or untested privacy law applications
  • Cross-jurisdictional issues involving multiple privacy regimes
  • International data sharing with countries outside Australia
  • Complex liability and contractual arrangements

Technical expertise

  • Advanced synthetic data generation techniques
  • Novel re-identification attack methodologies
  • Cutting-edge privacy-preserving technologies
  • Complex technical security assessments

Cultural expertise

  • Indigenous data governance and sovereignty
  • Cultural appropriateness of research methodologies
  • Community engagement and consultation processes
  • Culturally sensitive data interpretation and use

Note: The decision support tools provide guidance based on established frameworks and best practices. For situations involving significant legal, technical, or cultural complexity, organisations should seek appropriate expert consultation to ensure compliance and best outcomes.
