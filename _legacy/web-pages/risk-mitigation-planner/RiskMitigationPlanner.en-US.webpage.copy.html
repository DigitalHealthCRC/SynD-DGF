Implementation guidance

  1. Risk-technique matching: Select techniques based on specific re-identification risks identified
  2. Parameter tuning: Calibrate privacy parameters based on risk tolerance and utility requirements
  3. Validation testing: Conduct membership inference and other attack simulations
  4. Performance monitoring: Establish ongoing monitoring of privacy protection effectiveness
  5. Documentation: Maintain comprehensive documentation of technical implementation choices

üè¢ Organisational controls

‚ñº

Enhanced access controls

  • Role-based access: Implement granular access controls based on job requirements
  • Multi-factor authentication: Require MFA for all synthetic data access
  • Privileged access management: Additional controls for administrative access
  • Session monitoring: Log and monitor all data access sessions
  • Regular access reviews: Quarterly review and certification of access permissions

Staff training and awareness

  • Privacy training: Comprehensive privacy law and best practice training
  • Technical training: Training on synthetic data handling and protection measures
  • Incident response training: Regular drills and response procedure training
  • Cultural competency: Training on Indigenous data sovereignty and cultural sensitivities
  • Ongoing education: Regular updates on emerging privacy risks and technologies

Governance structures

  • Privacy committee: Dedicated committee with decision-making authority
  • Risk assessment process: Formal process for evaluating new privacy risks
  • Incident management: Clear procedures for privacy incident response
  • Third-party oversight: Enhanced due diligence for external service providers
  • Regular reporting: Privacy risk reporting to executive leadership

Enhanced contractual protections

  • Data processing agreements: Comprehensive agreements with specific privacy obligations
  • Liability provisions: Clear allocation of liability for privacy breaches
  • Audit rights: Rights to audit third-party compliance with privacy requirements
  • Data breach notification: Specific requirements for breach notification timelines
  • Data return/destruction: Clear obligations for data return or secure destruction

Regulatory compliance measures

  • Privacy impact assessments: Comprehensive PIAs with regular updates
  • Regulatory consultation: Proactive engagement with privacy regulators
  • Compliance monitoring: Regular assessment of compliance with applicable laws
  • Legal advice: Access to specialist privacy law expertise
  • Cross-jurisdictional analysis: Assessment of multi-jurisdictional requirements

Community and stakeholder protections

  • Community benefit agreements: Formal agreements on community benefits from research
  • Data sovereignty frameworks: Recognition of Indigenous data governance rights
  • Transparency measures: Public reporting on data use and privacy protections
  • Complaint mechanisms: Accessible processes for privacy complaints and concerns
  • Independent oversight: External oversight of privacy protection measures

  • Incident tracking: Systematic tracking and analysis of privacy incidents
  • Performance dashboards: Real-time monitoring of privacy and security controls

Assurance and audit

  • Internal audits: Regular internal privacy and security audits
  • External assessments: Independent privacy and security assessments
  • Penetration testing: Regular testing of security controls and defences
  • Compliance audits: Formal audits of regulatory compliance
  • Continuous improvement: Regular updates to controls based on audit findings
